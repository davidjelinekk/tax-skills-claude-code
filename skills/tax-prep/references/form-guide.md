# Tax Form Selection Guide

Which forms to file based on your entity type, income sources, and tax situation.

## Entity → Return Type

| Entity Type | Federal Return | IL Return | K-1 Issued? |
|-------------|---------------|-----------|-------------|
| Sole Proprietorship | Schedule C on 1040 | IL-1040 | No |
| Single-Member LLC (disregarded) | Schedule C on 1040 | IL-1040 | No |
| Multi-Member LLC (partnership) | Form 1065 | IL-1065 | Yes (K-1) |
| LLC → S-Corp election | Form 1120-S | IL-1120-ST | Yes (K-1) |
| C-Corporation | Form 1120 | IL-1120 | No (1099-DIV for dividends) |
| Trust/Estate | Form 1041 | IL-1041 | Yes (K-1) |

## Filing Scenarios

### Scenario 1: W-2 Employee Only
**Forms needed:**
- Form 1040
- Schedule A (if itemizing — mortgage interest, SALT, charitable)
- Schedule B (if interest/dividends > $1,500)
- IL-1040
- Schedule ICR (IL property tax and education credits)

### Scenario 2: Single-Member LLC (Sole Prop)
**Forms needed:**
- Form 1040
- Schedule C (business income/expenses)
- Schedule SE (self-employment tax)
- Schedule 1 (adjustments — SE tax deduction, SE health insurance)
- Form 8829 (home office, if using regular method)
- Form 4562 (depreciation, Section 179)
- Form 8995 or 8995-A (QBI deduction)
- Schedule A (if itemizing)
- IL-1040
- Schedule ICR (IL credits)

### Scenario 3: Multi-Member LLC (Partnership)
**Entity return:**
- Form 1065 (partnership return)
- Schedule K-1 (one per partner)
- Form 4562 (depreciation at entity level)
- IL-1065 (IL partnership return)
- Schedule K-1-P (IL, one per partner)
- Schedule PTE (if PTE tax elected)

**Each partner's individual return:**
- Form 1040
- Schedule E, Part II (K-1 income)
- Schedule SE (if general partner — SE income from K-1)
- Form 8995 or 8995-A (QBI deduction)
- IL-1040 (with PTE credit if applicable)

### Scenario 4: LLC with S-Corp Election
**Entity return:**
- Form 1120-S (S-Corp return)
- Schedule K-1 (one per shareholder)
- Form 4562 (depreciation)
- Form 941 (quarterly payroll — 4x/year)
- Form 940 (annual FUTA)
- W-2 (for shareholder-employees)
- IL-1120-ST (IL S-Corp return)
- Schedule PTE (if elected)

**Shareholder's individual return:**
- Form 1040
- Schedule E, Part II (K-1 income)
- Form 8995 or 8995-A (QBI deduction)
- Form 7203 (S-Corp shareholder basis tracking)
- IL-1040

### Scenario 5: Multiple LLCs + Personal
**Filing order (critical):**
1. Each entity return (1065 or 1120-S) — due March 15
2. Wait for K-1s from all entities
3. Individual Form 1040 — due April 15

**Individual return includes:**
- All K-1s reported on Schedule E
- Any Schedule C income (for disregarded LLCs)
- W-2 income
- Investment income (Schedules B, D, Form 8949)
- All applicable credits and deductions

### Scenario 6: Investment/Trading Income
**Forms needed:**
- Form 8949 (sales of capital assets — each transaction)
- Schedule D (capital gains/losses summary)
- Form 1099-B reconciliation
- Form 6781 (Section 1256 contracts — futures, broad-based index options)
- Schedule B (interest/dividends > $1,500)
- Form 8960 (Net Investment Income Tax if AGI > $200K/$250K)

**Crypto-specific:**
- Form 8949 (every sale, trade, exchange)
- Answer "Digital Assets" question on Form 1040 page 1
- Form 1099-DA (from exchanges, starting 2025-2026)

### Scenario 7: Rental Property
**Forms needed:**
- Schedule E, Part I (rental income/expenses)
- Form 4562 (depreciation of rental property)
- Form 8582 (passive activity loss limitations)
- Form 8582-CR (passive activity credit limitations)

**If claiming Real Estate Professional Status:**
- Time log documentation (not a form, but essential for audit defense)
- Grouping election statement (attached to return in first year)

## Supporting Forms Reference

| Form | When Needed | Purpose |
|------|------------|---------|
| Schedule 1 | Adjustments to income | SE tax deduction, SE health insurance, IRA, HSA, student loan interest, educator expenses |

| Schedule C | Sole prop/SMLLC | Business income and expenses |
| Schedule D | Capital gains/losses | Summary from Form 8949 |

| Schedule SE | Self-employment tax | 15.3% on net SE income |
| Form 4562 | Depreciation | Section 179, bonus depreciation, MACRS |
| Form 4797 | Business asset sales | Sale of business property (Section 1231 gains/losses) |
| Form 5695 | Energy credits | Residential clean energy, energy efficient property |
| Form 7203 | S-Corp basis | Required for S-Corp shareholders |
| Form 8283 | Noncash donations >$500 | Charitable property donations |
| Form 8582 | Passive activity limits | Passive loss limitation calculation |
| Form 8606 | Nondeductible IRA | Backdoor Roth tracking |
| Form 8829 | Home office | Regular method (actual expenses) |
| Form 8949 | Capital asset sales | Individual transaction detail |

## Extension Forms

| Entity | Extension Form | Effect |
|--------|---------------|--------|
| Individual (1040) | Form 4868 | 6 months (to Oct 15) |
| Partnership (1065) | Form 7004 | 6 months (to Sep 15) |
| S-Corp (1120-S) | Form 7004 | 6 months (to Sep 15) |
| C-Corp (1120) | Form 7004 | 6 months (to Oct 15) |
| IL Individual | Auto with federal | Must file IL by due date |

**IMPORTANT**: Extension extends time to FILE, not time to PAY.
Include estimated tax payment with extension to avoid penalties.

## Amended Return Forms

| Original Return | Amendment Form | Deadline |
|----------------|----------------|----------|
| Form 1040 | Form 1040-X | 3 years from filing or 2 years from payment |
| Form 1065 | Form 1065 (amended box) | 3 years from filing |
| Form 1120-S | Form 1120-S (amended box) | 3 years from filing |
| IL-1040 | IL-1040-X | 3 years, or 120 days after federal change |

## E-Filing Requirements
- Tax preparers filing 11+ returns MUST e-file
- Individuals can choose paper or e-file
- Form 1040-X can now be e-filed (current and prior 2 years)
- Partnership/S-Corp returns with 100+ K-1s must be e-filed
