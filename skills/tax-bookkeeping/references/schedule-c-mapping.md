# Schedule C Line-by-Line Transaction Mapping Guide

Quick reference for categorizing common business transactions to the correct
IRS Schedule C line (also applicable to Form 1065 and 1120-S deductions).

## Transaction → IRS Line Mapping

### Advertising (Line 8)
- Google Ads / Meta Ads / LinkedIn Ads spend
- Business cards, brochures, flyers
- Website design, hosting, domain registration
- SEO services, content marketing
- Trade show booth fees and display materials
- Promotional merchandise (branded swag)
- Social media management services
- Email marketing platform (Mailchimp, etc.)
- Print advertising (magazines, newspapers)
- Signage and banners
- Online directory listings (Yelp, Google Business)

### Car and Truck Expenses (Line 9)
**Choose ONE method per vehicle per year:**

**Standard Mileage Rate:**
- Business miles x IRS rate ($0.70/mile for 2025)
- Plus parking and tolls (always deductible on top)
- Requires contemporaneous mileage log

**Actual Expenses (business % of):**
- Gas and fuel
- Oil changes and maintenance
- Tires
- Insurance
- Registration
- Loan interest
- Lease payments
- Car washes
- Repairs

**NEVER deductible:** Commuting miles (home to regular office), traffic tickets

### Commissions and Fees (Line 10)
- Stripe/PayPal/Square processing fees (typically 2.9% + $0.30)
- Referral fees paid to partners
- Sales commissions paid to agents
- Platform marketplace fees (Shopify, Amazon, Etsy)
- Affiliate program payouts
- Credit card surcharges

### Contract Labor (Line 11)
- Payments to freelancers/independent contractors
- Subcontractor work
- Virtual assistant services
- Outsourced development/design
- **Must issue 1099-NEC if $600+ paid to any individual in tax year**
- Payments to corporations are generally exempt from 1099 requirement (except attorneys and medical/healthcare)

### Depreciation and Section 179 (Line 13)
**Capitalize and depreciate (or expense) items over $2,500:**
- Computers and laptops
- Monitors, printers, peripherals
- Office furniture (desks, chairs)
- Business vehicles (subject to luxury auto limits)
- Photography/video equipment
- Server hardware
- Machinery and tools
- Software licenses (perpetual, not subscription)

**Section 179 election**: Expense up to $2,500,000 (2025) in year placed in service
**Bonus depreciation**: 100% (permanent post-OBBBA) for new and used property

### Employee Benefits (Line 14)
- Health/dental/vision insurance premiums for **employees** (not owner in sole prop)
- Life insurance premiums for employees
- Workers' compensation insurance
- Employee retirement plan contributions (employer match)
- Dependent care assistance
- **Owner's health insurance**: Deducted on Form 1040, Schedule 1, Line 17 (not here)

### Insurance (Line 15)
- General liability insurance
- Professional liability / Errors & Omissions (E&O)
- Product liability insurance
- Business property insurance
- Cyber liability insurance
- Business interruption insurance
- Commercial auto insurance (if using actual expense method)
- Key person insurance
- **NOT here**: Health insurance (Line 14 for employees, Form 1040 for owner)

### Interest (Lines 16a and 16b)
**16a — Mortgage interest on business property:**
- Commercial mortgage interest
- Land/building loan interest for business property

**16b — Other business interest:**
- Business credit card interest
- Business line of credit interest
- SBA loan interest
- Equipment financing interest
- Vehicle loan interest (if using actual expense method)
- **NOT deductible**: Personal credit card interest, home mortgage (goes to Schedule A or Form 8829)

### Legal and Professional Services (Line 17)
- Attorney fees (business-related)
- CPA/accountant fees
- Bookkeeping service fees
- Tax preparation fees (business portion)
- Consulting fees (non-contractor — one-time advisory)
- Payroll service fees (ADP, Gusto, etc.)
- Business entity formation fees
- Trademark/patent attorney fees

### Office Expenses (Line 18)
- Paper, toner, ink cartridges
- Pens, notebooks, office supplies
- Postage and stamps
- Envelopes, mailing supplies
- Printer maintenance
- Small equipment under $2,500 (de minimis safe harbor)
- Software subscriptions if under $2,500/year
- Cleaning supplies for office

### Pension and Profit-Sharing Plans (Line 19)
- SEP-IRA employer contributions
- SIMPLE IRA employer match
- Solo 401(k) employer contributions
- Defined benefit plan contributions
- **For sole proprietors**: Owner's contributions may go on Form 1040 instead (SEP, Solo 401k)
- **For S-Corps**: Employer contributions for shareholder-employees go here

### Rent — Equipment (Line 20a)
- Copier/printer leases
- Equipment rental
- Tool rental
- Vehicle leases (if not using mileage rate)
- Computer/server leases

### Rent — Other Business Property (Line 20b)
- Office rent
- Warehouse/storage rent
- Coworking space membership (WeWork, etc.)
- Retail/storefront rent
- Parking space rental (for business property)
- PO Box rental

### Repairs and Maintenance (Line 22)
- Equipment repairs
- Computer/IT repairs
- Software maintenance contracts
- HVAC maintenance (business property)
- Plumbing/electrical repairs (business property)
- Pest control (business property)
- **General home repairs**: Only business % deductible via Form 8829

### Supplies (Line 22)
- Materials consumed in providing services
- Cleaning supplies
- Safety supplies
- Packaging materials
- **Distinct from inventory**: Supplies are consumed, not resold

### Taxes and Licenses (Line 23)
- State/local business license fees
- Business registration fees
- Professional license fees
- Employer payroll taxes (FICA employer share, FUTA, SUTA)
- IL replacement tax (1.5% for partnerships/S-Corps)
- Personal property tax (on business assets)
- Real estate tax (on business property)
- **NOT here**: Federal income tax (never deductible), self-employment tax (deducted on 1040)

### Travel (Line 24a)
- Airfare (business travel)
- Hotel/lodging (business travel)
- Taxi/Uber/Lyft (business travel)
- Rental car (business travel)
- Baggage fees
- Wi-Fi charges during travel
- Laundry/dry cleaning during extended business travel
- Tips for service during travel
- **Travel must be primarily business**: Domestic: >50% of days are business days

### Meals (Line 24b) — 50% Deductible
- Client meals (business discussion required)
- Team meals during business meetings
- Meals while traveling overnight for business
- Coffee/snacks at client meetings
- **Must document**: Amount, date, location, business purpose, who attended
- **NOT deductible**: Personal meals, general snacks for office (unless for employees/events)

### Other Expenses (Line 27)
**Catch-all for legitimate business expenses not fitting Lines 8-26:**
- Bank service charges and wire fees
- SaaS/cloud subscriptions (if not on Line 18)
- Education and training (business-related courses, certifications)
- Books and publications (business-related)
- Professional dues and memberships
- Telephone and internet (business %)
- Shipping and delivery costs
- Background check fees
- Drug testing fees
- Uniforms and work clothing (must not be suitable for everyday wear)
- Moving expenses for business equipment

### Business Use of Home (Line 30)
Two methods:

**Simplified Method:**
- $5 per square foot, max 300 sq ft = **$1,500 max**
- No Form 8829 needed
- Cannot create a loss

**Regular Method (Form 8829):**
- Calculate business % = Office sq ft / Total home sq ft
- Apply % to: mortgage interest, property tax, utilities, insurance, repairs, depreciation
- Can carry forward unused deduction
- Depreciation recapture on home sale (25% rate)

## Split Transaction Rules

When a transaction is partly personal and partly business:

1. **Cell phone**: Deduct business % only (e.g., 70% business = deduct 70%)
2. **Home internet**: Business % via home office or reasonable allocation
3. **Vehicle**: Business miles only (never commuting)
4. **Travel with personal days**: Allocate costs — domestic travel must be >50% business days
5. **Meals**: Only the business portion; split bills if personal guests included

**Always document the split ratio and basis for the allocation.**
