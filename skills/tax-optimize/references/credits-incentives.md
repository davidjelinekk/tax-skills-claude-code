# Credits & Incentives — R&D Credit, ERC, Energy Credits, ACA Premium Credit

## R&D Tax Credit (IRC Section 41)

### Qualifying Activities — 4-Part Test
ALL four tests must be satisfied:

1. **Permitted purpose**: Develop new or improved product, process, software, technique, formula, or invention
2. **Technological in nature**: Relies on principles of engineering, computer science, biological science, or physical science
3. **Elimination of uncertainty**: Must be uncertainty regarding capability, method, or design at the outset
4. **Process of experimentation**: Systematic process to evaluate alternatives (modeling, simulation, trial and error, testing)

### What Qualifies (Common Examples)
- Developing new software features or architecture
- Improving manufacturing processes
- Designing new products or prototypes
- Developing algorithms or data models
- Testing and quality assurance of new designs
- Environmental testing and compliance R&D
- Cloud infrastructure optimization (if technically uncertain)

### What Does NOT Qualify
- Market research or consumer testing
- Routine data collection
- Adaptation of existing products without technical uncertainty
- Management studies or efficiency surveys
- Foreign research (must be conducted in the US)
- Research after commercial production begins (post-production)
- Social science, arts, or humanities research

### Qualified Research Expenses (QREs)
- **Wages**: W-2 wages for employees performing, supervising, or supporting qualified research
- **Supplies**: Materials consumed or used in research (not capital equipment)
- **Contract research**: 65% of amounts paid to third parties for qualified research
- **Basic research payments**: 75% of payments to qualified organizations (universities)

### Credit Calculation — Two Methods

**Regular Credit (RC)**:
- 20% of QREs exceeding a base amount
- Base amount = fixed-base percentage x average annual gross receipts (prior 4 years)
- Complex calculation — rarely used by small businesses

**Alternative Simplified Credit (ASC)** — Most Common:
- **14%** of QREs exceeding 50% of average QREs for prior 3 years
- If no QREs in prior 3 years: 6% of current year QREs
- Simpler, often produces a smaller but more defensible credit
- Election made annually on Form 6765

### Small Business Payroll Tax Offset
- **Eligibility**: Gross receipts < $5M AND less than 5 years of gross receipts
- **Offset**: Apply R&D credit against employer FICA (Social Security portion)
- **Limit**: Up to **$500,000 per year** (OBBBA increased from $250,000)
- **Duration**: Available for up to **5 years** (OBBBA expansion)
- Claim on Form 6765 Part III + Form 8974 with payroll
- **Strategy**: Pre-revenue startups can bank R&D credits against future payroll taxes

### Pass-Through Entities (LLCs, S-Corps, Partnerships)
- Credit is calculated at the entity level
- Flows through to owners/shareholders on **Schedule K-1**
- Each owner claims their share on Form 3800 (General Business Credit)
- Cannot reduce tax below tentative minimum tax (AMT consideration)

### Section 174 Amortization (Post-2022)
- **Required**: Domestic R&D expenses must be amortized over **5 years** (not expensed)
- Foreign R&D: amortized over **15 years**
- Applies to all Section 174 expenditures (broader than Section 41 QREs)
- **Major cash flow impact**: Cannot deduct R&D expenses in the year incurred
- R&D credit still available in addition to amortized deduction
- OBBBA did NOT repeal the amortization requirement

### Documentation Requirements
- **Contemporaneous records**: time tracking, project descriptions, technical narratives
- Document the 4-part test for each project
- Retain: lab notebooks, design documents, meeting notes, emails, test results
- **IRS scrutiny is increasing** — do not claim without proper documentation

### Form 6765
- Part I: Regular credit calculation
- Part II: Alternative simplified credit
- Part III: Payroll tax election for qualified small businesses
- Attach to business return (1120-S, 1065) or individual return (1040 Schedule C)

## Employee Retention Credit (ERC) — Status & Warnings

### Program Timeline
- **2020**: 50% of qualified wages up to $10,000/employee/year = max $5,000/employee
- **Q1-Q3 2021**: 70% of qualified wages up to $10,000/employee/quarter = max $7,000/quarter
- **Q4 2021**: Retroactively terminated for most businesses (Infrastructure Investment and Jobs Act)
- **Exception**: Recovery startup businesses could claim ERC through Q4 2021

### Current Status (2025-2026)
- **IRS moratorium on processing new ERC claims** began September 2023
- IRS has processed some claims but continues to hold most filed after the moratorium
- **Voluntary Disclosure Program**: For businesses that received improper ERC and want to return funds with reduced penalties
- **IRS Withdrawal Program**: For businesses that filed but haven't received payment — can withdraw the claim

### Audit Risk
- IRS has flagged ERC as one of its **top enforcement priorities**
- Thousands of improper claims driven by aggressive "ERC mills"
- IRS sending disallowance letters for clearly ineligible claims
- Criminal investigations underway for fraudulent promoters
- **Audit risk is HIGH for 2020-2021 ERC claims**

### Guidance
- **Do NOT pursue new ERC claims** without experienced tax counsel
- If you previously claimed ERC, ensure documentation supports eligibility
- Eligibility required EITHER: government orders partially/fully suspending operations OR significant decline in gross receipts
- PPP interaction: same wages cannot be used for both PPP forgiveness and ERC
- **If you received ERC through a "mill" promoter**: consult a CPA immediately about voluntary disclosure

## Energy Credits (Post-OBBBA)

### Residential Clean Energy Credit (Section 25D) — Form 5695 Part I
- **30% credit** (no cap) for qualified clean energy property
- Effective through 2034 (phases down to 26% in 2033, 22% in 2034)

**Qualifying property**:
- Solar electric (photovoltaic) panels
- Solar water heating
- Wind energy (small residential turbines)
- Geothermal heat pumps
- Battery storage technology (3 kWh+ capacity)
- Fuel cell property (max $500/0.5 kW)

**Rules**:
- Must be installed on taxpayer's primary or secondary residence in the US
- New construction AND existing homes qualify
- Nonrefundable credit — can carry forward unused credit
- Rental properties do NOT qualify (use business energy credits instead)

### Energy Efficient Home Improvement Credit (Section 25C) — Form 5695 Part II
- **30%** of costs, with per-item and annual limits

**Annual limits** (reset each tax year):
- **$3,200 total annual cap** broken into:
  - $2,000 for heat pumps, heat pump water heaters, biomass stoves
  - $1,200 for all other improvements (windows, doors, insulation, etc.)

**Per-item limits**:

| Improvement | Credit Limit |
|-------------|-------------|
| Exterior windows/skylights | $600 total |
| Exterior doors | $250/door, $500 total |
| Insulation/air sealing | $1,200 total (within $1,200 category) |
| Central A/C | $600 |
| Heat pump (HVAC) | $2,000 |
| Heat pump water heater | $2,000 |
| Biomass stove | $2,000 |
| Home energy audit | $150 |
| Electrical panel upgrade | $600 |

**Rules**:
- Must meet Energy Star or IECC standards (varies by product)
- Principal residence only (not rental or second homes)
- Annual limit resets — can spread improvements across years for maximum credits

### Clean Vehicle Credit (Section 30D) — New EVs
- Up to **$7,500** for qualifying new electric vehicles
- Two components: $3,750 for critical minerals requirement + $3,750 for battery components requirement
- **MSRP limits**: $80,000 (SUVs/trucks/vans), $55,000 (sedans/other)
- **Income limits**: $300,000 (MFJ), $225,000 (HOH), $150,000 (single)
- Can transfer credit to dealer at point of sale (instant discount)
- Vehicle must be assembled in North America
- List of qualifying vehicles at fueleconomy.gov

### Used Clean Vehicle Credit (Section 25E)
- Up to **$4,000** or 30% of sale price (whichever is less)
- Must be purchased from a licensed dealer (not private sale)
- **Price limit**: $25,000 or less
- **Income limits**: $150,000 (MFJ), $112,500 (HOH), $75,000 (single)
- Vehicle must be at least 2 model years old
- Only one used EV credit per vehicle lifetime

### Business Energy Credits

**Investment Tax Credit (ITC) — Section 48**:
- Base credit: **6%** of qualified energy property cost
- **Bonus credit: 30%** if prevailing wage AND apprenticeship requirements met
- Additional 10% for domestic content; 10% for energy communities
- Applies to: solar, wind, geothermal, battery storage, biogas, microgrid controllers
- 5-year recapture period (partial recapture if property ceases to qualify)

**Production Tax Credit (PTC) — Section 45**:
- Per-kWh credit for electricity produced from qualified sources
- Base rate: 0.3 cents/kWh (2025, indexed for inflation)
- Bonus rate: **1.5 cents/kWh** with prevailing wage/apprenticeship
- 10-year credit period from date facility placed in service
- Applies to: wind, solar (if PTC elected over ITC), geothermal, biomass, hydropower

**Choice**: Solar projects can elect either ITC or PTC (not both). PTC generally favors large-scale installations with high capacity factors.

### Commercial Clean Vehicle Credit (Section 45W)
- Up to **$7,500** (vehicles <14,000 lbs GVWR) or **$40,000** (vehicles 14,000+ lbs GVWR)
- 15% of basis for PHEVs, 30% of basis for full EVs
- No MSRP or income limits (business credit)
- Vehicle must be used in a trade or business
- No North American assembly requirement

## Adoption Credit (Section 36C)
- Up to **$17,280** per eligible child (2025, indexed for inflation)
- Nonrefundable, but unused credit carries forward **5 years**
- Income phase-out: $259,190-$299,190 MAGI (2025)
- Qualifying expenses: adoption fees, court costs, attorney fees, travel, re-adoption of foreign child
- **Special needs adoption**: full credit regardless of actual expenses
- Claimed on Form 8839
- Cannot claim for surrogate parenting or adopting spouse's child
- Employer-provided adoption assistance: up to $17,280 excluded from income (same limits)

## Education Credits (AOTC and Lifetime Learning)

### American Opportunity Tax Credit (AOTC)
- Up to **$2,500 per student** per year (100% of first $2,000 + 25% of next $2,000)
- **40% refundable** (up to $1,000 refundable even with zero tax liability)
- First **4 years** of post-secondary education only
- Student must be enrolled at least half-time pursuing a degree
- Income phase-out: $80,000-$90,000 (single), $160,000-$180,000 (MFJ)
- Cannot claim if filing MFS
- Qualifying expenses: tuition, required fees, course materials (even if not paid to school)
- Cannot use same expenses for both AOTC and 529 tax-free withdrawal

### Lifetime Learning Credit (LLC)
- Up to **$2,000 per tax return** (not per student) — 20% of up to $10,000 in expenses
- **No limit** on number of years claimed
- Available for undergraduate, graduate, professional, AND continuing education
- Student does NOT need to be pursuing a degree
- One or more courses qualifies (no minimum enrollment)
- Income phase-out: $80,000-$90,000 (single), $160,000-$180,000 (MFJ)
- Nonrefundable
- Cannot claim if filing MFS

### AOTC vs LLC Quick Comparison

| Feature | AOTC | LLC |
|---------|------|-----|
| Max credit | $2,500/student | $2,000/return |
| Refundable | 40% (up to $1,000) | No |
| Years available | First 4 years | Unlimited |
| Enrollment | Half-time minimum | One course |
| Degree required | Yes | No |
| Best for | Undergrad years 1-4 | Graduate, professional, continuing ed |

**Cannot claim both AOTC and LLC for the same student in the same year.**

## Work Opportunity Tax Credit (WOTC)
- Federal credit for hiring from targeted groups: veterans, SNAP recipients, ex-felons, vocational rehabilitation referrals, summer youth employees, long-term unemployment recipients
- Credit: 25% of first-year wages (if 120-399 hours) or 40% (if 400+ hours), up to **$6,000** of qualified wages per employee (up to $9,600 for certain disabled veterans)
- Must file Form 8850 (pre-screening) with state workforce agency within **28 days** of hire
- Claimed on Form 5884
- Nonrefundable (but can be carried back 1 year, forward 20 years)

## ACA Premium Tax Credit (Section 36B) — Form 8962

### How It Works
- Subsidizes health insurance premiums for marketplace (exchange) plans
- Based on household income relative to Federal Poverty Level (FPL)
- Available to those earning **100%-400% FPL** (OBBBA extended enhanced credits)
- Enhanced credits (originally from ARP): no cliff at 400% FPL — premium capped at 8.5% of income

### Modified AGI for ACA
ACA uses its own income definition:
- AGI (line 11 of Form 1040)
- PLUS tax-exempt interest income
- PLUS excluded foreign income (Form 2555)
- Does NOT include: gifts, inheritances, tax-exempt Social Security

### Reconciliation at Filing (Form 8962)
- If advance credits were paid to insurer: **must reconcile** on tax return
- **Actual income > estimated**: Repay excess advance credits

**Repayment caps (2025)**:

| Income (% of FPL) | Single | MFJ |
|-------------------|--------|-----|
| Below 200% | $375 | $750 |
| 200%-300% | $975 | $1,950 |
| 300%-400% | $1,625 | $3,250 |
| Above 400% | Full repayment (no cap) | Full repayment |

- **Actual income < estimated**: Receive additional credit as refund

### Self-Employment Interaction
- SE income affects premium subsidy eligibility
- SE health insurance deduction interacts with PTC (circular calculation — iterative)
- IRS provides iterative calculation method in Pub 974

### MAGI Reduction Strategies for Larger Subsidies
1. **Maximize retirement contributions**: Solo 401(k), SEP IRA reduce AGI
2. **HSA contributions**: Above-the-line deduction reduces AGI
3. **Section 179/bonus depreciation**: Reduce business income
4. **Tax loss harvesting**: Realize investment losses to offset gains
5. **Timing**: Defer income to future years if possible
6. **Roth conversions**: Do NOT help — add to MAGI

### Key Deadlines
- Open enrollment: November 1 through January 15 (varies by state)
- Special enrollment: qualifying life event triggers 60-day window
- Form 8962 due with tax return — **failure to reconcile = no advance credits next year**

### Common Mistakes
- Not reporting life changes (income, household size) to marketplace during the year
- Forgetting to file Form 8962 (IRS blocks future advance credits)
- Not accounting for capital gains spikes when estimating income
- Ignoring SE income fluctuations in estimated income
