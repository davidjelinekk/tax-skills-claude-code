# Illinois Tax Guide for LLCs and Individuals

## Illinois Income Tax Rate
- **Individual rate**: Flat **4.95%**
- **Corporate rate**: 7.0% + 2.5% replacement tax = 9.5% total
- **No local income tax** in Illinois

## Illinois Replacement Tax (Personal Property Tax Replacement Income Tax)

| Entity Type | Rate |
|-------------|------|
| Partnerships / Multi-member LLCs | **1.5%** |
| S-Corporations | **1.5%** |
| C-Corporations | **2.5%** |
| Single-member LLC (disregarded) | None at entity level (reported on owner's IL-1040) |

- Filed on IL-1065 (partnerships) or IL-1120-ST (S-Corps)
- Due date: Same as federal (March 15 for partnerships/S-Corps)

## Pass-Through Entity (PTE) Tax Election

### How It Works
- S-Corps and partnerships elect to pay IL income tax at entity level at **4.95%**
- Enacted under **35 ILCS 5/201(p)**
- Entity takes deduction at entity level, reducing pass-through income
- Owners receive credit on individual IL return for their share of PTE tax paid

### Federal Benefit
- PTE tax is deducted as a business expense at the entity level per **IRS Notice 2020-75**
- This deduction is **NOT subject to the $10,000/$40,000 SALT cap**
- Effectively bypasses the SALT limitation for pass-through business income

### Post-OBBBA Considerations (2025+)
Even with SALT cap raised to $40,000:
1. PTE deduction is above-the-line (business expense) vs. below-the-line (itemized)
2. Reduces self-employment income for partners
3. Allows standard deduction + PTE deduction (double benefit)
4. Still valuable when total SALT exceeds the cap

### Election Rules
- Made annually on entity return
- Irrevocable after extended due date for that year
- Estimated payments required quarterly if PTE tax exceeds $500
- Available for tax years ending on/after Dec 31, 2021
- **Check**: Verify IL has extended PTE election beyond current sunset date

## Illinois Individual Return (IL-1040)

### Key Differences from Federal
- Illinois starts with federal AGI and makes IL-specific adjustments
- Add-backs: Federally exempt interest from other states' bonds
- Subtractions: U.S. government interest (Treasury bonds), IL retirement income, military pay
- **IL does NOT allow** itemized deductions — the IL personal exemption ($2,475 per person in 2025) is used instead

### IL Personal Exemption
- **$2,475 per person** (taxpayer, spouse, each dependent) — 2025
- This replaces the federal standard/itemized deduction concept
- Multiplied by number of exemptions claimed

### Credits Available on IL-1040

| Credit | Amount | Details |
|--------|--------|---------|
| Property Tax Credit | 5% of property taxes paid | Schedule ICR, nonrefundable |
| K-12 Education Credit | 25% of expenses over $250 | Max $750/household, Schedule ICR |
| Earned Income Credit | 20% of federal EITC | Refundable |
| PTE Tax Credit | Share of entity PTE tax paid | Dollar-for-dollar credit |
| Child Tax Credit (IL) | Check current year | Recently enacted |

### Schedule ICR (Credits)
- Property Tax Credit: 5% of property taxes on principal residence
- Education Expense Credit: 25% of qualifying K-12 expenses exceeding $250
  - Qualifying expenses: tuition, book rentals, lab fees
  - Maximum credit: $750 per household
  - Student must be full-time K-12, under age 21

## Illinois Partnership Return (IL-1065)

### Who Files
- Multi-member LLCs taxed as partnerships
- Due date: March 15 (15th day of 3rd month after fiscal year end)
- Extension: 6-month automatic (to September 15) using IL-1065-V

### Required Schedules
- Schedule K-1-P issued to each member
- Schedule B (IL apportionment, if applicable)
- Schedule PTE (if PTE tax election made)
- Replacement tax calculation (1.5% of net income)

### Annual Report
- LLCs must file Annual Report with IL Secretary of State
- **$75 filing fee** (due on anniversary of LLC formation)
- Separate from tax return — failure to file can result in administrative dissolution

## Illinois Sales Tax

### Rates
- State base rate: **6.25%**
- Local additions vary by municipality
- Chicago metro total: typically **8.0-10.25%**
- Must register and collect if selling tangible personal property in IL

### When LLCs Must Collect
- Selling tangible goods (not services) in Illinois
- Exceeding economic nexus threshold ($100,000 in sales or 200 transactions)
- Register with IL Department of Revenue for sales tax collection

## Illinois Unemployment Insurance (SUTA)
- New employer rate: approximately **3.175%** (varies by year)
- Taxable wage base: **$13,590** per employee (2025)
- Must register if you have employees in Illinois

## 120-Day Federal Change Rule
**CRITICAL**: If the IRS adjusts your federal return, you must file an amended IL return (IL-1040-X) within **120 days** of the federal final determination. Failure to do so extends the Illinois statute of limitations **indefinitely** for that issue.

## 529 Plan (Bright Start)
- IL tax deduction: Up to **$10,000/individual** or **$20,000/couple** per beneficiary per year
- At 4.95% rate = up to **$495** (individual) / **$990** (couple) tax savings per beneficiary

## Chicago and Cook County Taxes
- **Chicago Head Tax**: abolished in 2014. No current Chicago-specific employee head tax.
- **Chicago Lease Tax**: 9% on leases of personal property in Chicago (SaaS companies watch out — cloud computing/SaaS may be subject to this as "nonpossessory computer lease")
- **Chicago Amusement Tax**: 9% on streaming services (Netflix, Spotify, etc.) — called the "Netflix tax"
- **Cook County Sweetened Beverage Tax**: repealed 2017
- **Chicago Personal Property Lease Transaction Tax**: applies to many digital services
- **Cook County property tax rates**: vary by municipality, typically 1.5%-2.5% of assessed value (assessment at 10% of market value for residential in Cook County)
- **Chicago business license**: varies by type ($250-$500+ for most business types)
- No city income tax in Chicago or any IL municipality
- **Cook County Sales Tax**: additional 1.75% on top of IL state rate (total in Chicago: ~10.25%)

## Chicago/Cook County Considerations for LLCs
- If LLC has physical presence or customers in Chicago, may be subject to city taxes
- SaaS businesses: Chicago Lease Tax potentially applies to cloud/SaaS revenue from Chicago customers
- Consult city of Chicago Department of Finance for specific obligations
- Annual compliance: Chicago business license renewal
